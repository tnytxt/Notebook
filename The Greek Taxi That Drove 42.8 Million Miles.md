Imagine driving the **same car for 4.6 million kilometers (2.8 million miles)** —the equivalent of **circling the Earth 115 times**. That’s exactly what **Gregorios Sachinidis**, a Greek taxi driver, accomplished with his **1976 Mercedes-Benz 240D**. His legendary vehicle became **one of the highest-mileage cars ever recorded**, proving that **meticulous maintenance and engineering excellence** can make a car last for decades.

In recognition of this **remarkable feat**, Mercedes-Benz took Sachinidis’ car to **their museum in Stuttgart**, immortalising it as **one of the longest-running diesel engines in history**. But how did this taxi survive nearly **two and a half decades** of non-stop driving? Let’s explore the **secrets behind this record-breaking car**.

## The Legendary Mercedes-Benz 240D: Built to Last

![](https://engineerine.com/wp-content/webp-express/webp-images/uploads/2025/02/LATEST-2025-02-11T000930.749-1024x538.png.webp)

The **Mercedes-Benz W115 240D**, part of the **iconic W115 series**, was one of the most **durable and well-built diesel cars** of its time. Known for its **simple yet robust design**, the 240D quickly gained a reputation for:

- **Reliable diesel engine**, designed for long-distance driving.
- **Minimal electronic components**, reducing the risk of mechanical failures.
- **Strong steel body**, built to withstand harsh conditions.
- **Fuel efficiency**, making it perfect for taxi services worldwide.

While most cars struggle to last beyond **300,000 kilometers (186,000 miles)** without major repairs, Sachinidis’ 240D went **beyond 4.6 million kilometers**, making it one of the most **resilient cars in history**.

## 23 Years of Relentless Driving

![](https://engineerine.com/wp-content/webp-express/webp-images/uploads/2025/02/LATEST-2025-02-11T001425362_enhanced-1024x538.png.webp)

From **1976 to 1999**, Gregorios Sachinidis used this **Mercedes-Benz 240D as a taxi in Greece**, often driving **over 600 kilometers (370 miles) daily**. His job required **long highway trips and constant urban driving**, yet the car **never failed him**.

Despite **financial struggles**, Sachinidis **never replaced the vehicle**, relying instead on **regular maintenance and hands-on repairs** to keep it running smoothly. His dedication turned this **ordinary diesel sedan into an automotive legend**.

## The Secret to 4.6 Million Kilometers: Unmatched Maintenance

What allowed Sachinidis’ car to outlast almost every other vehicle? **Consistent and detailed maintenance**. Some of the **key factors** behind the car’s longevity include:

- **Oil changes every 10,000 kilometers (6,200 miles)** to keep the diesel engine clean.
- **Frequent air and fuel filter replacements**, preventing contamination from damaging the engine.
- **Regular clutch and transmission checks**, ensuring smooth gear shifts.
- **Cooling system flushes**, preventing overheating.
- **Engine rebuilds when necessary**, extending its life instead of replacing it.

Unlike modern cars filled with **complex electronics**, the **240D’s mechanical simplicity** made it easy to repair, contributing to its **unparalleled longevity**.

## Mercedes-Benz Recognizes the World-Record Car

![](https://engineerine.com/wp-content/webp-express/webp-images/uploads/2025/02/Mercedes-Benz-Recognizes-the-World-Record-Car-1024x538.png.webp)

In **1999**, after 23 years of service, **Mercedes-Benz invited Gregorios Sachinidis to Stuttgart, Germany**, where they honored his **4.6-million-kilometer taxi**. The company recognized his **dedication to their brand** and requested the **historic 240D for their museum**, where it remains a **testament to German engineering**.

As a gesture of appreciation, **Mercedes gifted Sachinidis a brand-new E-Class**, ensuring he could continue driving in **a modern luxury vehicle**.

Sachinidis’ **240D set a precedent**, and today, many Mercedes taxis are still running **beyond a million kilometers**.

## Could Modern Cars Last This Long?

With **advanced technology, hybrid engines, and electric vehicles**, could a **modern car reach 4.6 million kilometers** like the **240D**? The answer is **complicated**. While modern cars have:

- **More efficient engines**
- **Better aerodynamics**
- **Advanced materials**

They also have **more electronic components**, which are difficult to repair and expensive to replace. **Most modern vehicles are not designed to last for decades**, making it unlikely for them to **match the mileage of the 240D**.

However, **with regular maintenance, hybrid and electric taxis** might be the next record holders in the future.
