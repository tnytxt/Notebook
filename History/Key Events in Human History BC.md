#### Prehistoric Era
- **200,000 B.C.** — Emergence of Homo sapiens in Africa  
- **62,000 B.C.** — Bow and arrows with stone points used  
- **30,000 B.C.** — Cro‑Magnon humans create cave paintings and spread across Europe  
- **25,000 B.C.** — Humans cross the Bering Strait into the Americas  
- **18,000 B.C.** — Clay pottery and early use of raw metals begin  
- **10,000 B.C.** — Humans reach South America's southernmost point; agriculture begins
#### Neolithic Revolution & Early Civilisations
- **8,000 B.C.** — Domestication of animals and crops in the Fertile Crescent; earliest domesticated dog (Idaho, c. 8400 B.C.)  
- **7,000 B.C.** — Major earthquake damages Jericho  
- **6,500 B.C.** — Rising seas isolate Britain from continental Europe  
- **6,000 B.C.** — Beer brewed in the Near East  
- **5,500 B.C.** — Settled societies in Mesopotamia; early Bronze Age developments begin
#### Bronze Age Innovations
- **4,500 B.C.** — Plough introduced; copper and bronze metallurgy advance  
- **4,242 B.C.** — First year recorded on the Egyptian civil calendar  
- **4,000 B.C.** — Invention of the wheel; rice cultivation spreads in China  
- **3,400 B.C.** — Proto‑writing systems appear; ziggurats constructed in Mesopotamia
#### Early Empires
- **3,100 B.C.** — First Dynasty unites Upper and Lower Egypt  
- **2,750 B.C.** — Reign of Gilgamesh in Uruk; (Stonehenge construction begins c. 2400 B.C.)  
- **1,792 B.C.** — Hammurabi rules Babylon  
- **1,766 B.C.** — Traditional start of the Shang dynasty in China  
- **c. 1250–1200 B.C.** — Traditional dating for the Exodus (Moses leads Israelites from Egypt)
#### Iron Age Developments
- **814 B.C.** — Carthage founded by Phoenician settlers  
- **776 B.C.** — First recorded Olympic Games in Greece  
- **221–214 B.C.** — Qin Shi Huang unifies China; construction of sections of the Great Wall begins
#### Roman Era
- **63–53 B.C.** — Roman campaigns in Judea; Battle of Carrhae between Rome and Parthia (53 B.C.)  
- **44 B.C.** — Julius Caesar assassinated  
- **27 B.C.** — Octavian (Augustus) becomes first Roman emperor
#### Transition to the Common Era
- **5 B.C.** — Traditional date for the birth of Jesus in Bethlehem
