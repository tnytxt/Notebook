1. Use contractions:
   - Instead of "cannot," use "can't."
   - Instead of "would not," use "wouldn't."

2. Use simpler words:
   - Instead of "utilize," use "use."
   - Instead of "endeavor," use "try."

3. Use an active voice:
   - Instead of "The ball was thrown by the boy,"
   - Use "The boy threw the ball."

4. Use personal pronouns:
   - Instead of "The customer," use "You."

5. Use positive language:
   - Instead of "Don't forget," use "Remember."

6. Use humor:
   - A well-placed joke can make your writing more engaging.

7. Use storytelling:
   - Share personal stories to connect with your readers.

8. Use visuals:
   - Images, infographics, and videos can help break up your text and make it more visually appealing.

9. Use strong verbs:
   - Instead of "said," use "exclaimed," "whispered," or "shouted."

10. Use strong adjectives and adverbs:
   - Instead of "good," use "excellent," "fantastic," or "wonderful."

Here is an example of how to make text more friendly:

Original:
> The utilization of complex algorithms has enabled the development of a novel solution to the problem of data redundancy.

Friendly:
> We used smart math to create a new way to fix data problems.

By following these tips, you can make your writing more friendly and engaging.
