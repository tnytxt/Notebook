Spots, unpleasant odours, and tissue defects can lead to your favourite clothes ending up in the garbage.
But why not try to restore them to their original appearance? Here are 9 tips on how to remove various stains, eliminate unpleasant smells, and
extend the life of your garments.

#### 1. What to Do If the Neck is Stretched
>Give the neck of the t-shirt boiling water.

If you have T-shirts, sweatshirts, or long sleeves with a stretched neck, try this trick: Boil water in a pot, then gently immerse the neck in boiling water for a few seconds.
Once removed, the neck will be denser and return to its original form.

#### 2. Get Rust Stains Off Your Clothes?
>You'll need citric acid.

To remove rust stains, mix 100 ml of water with 30 grams of citric acid. Wet a sponge in the solution and apply it to the stained area.
Let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then wash the item with detergent.
**Note:** Treat stain from periphery to the centre to prevent spreading.

#### 3. Wash Away Traces of Fat?
>Use a dishwashing detergent.

Fat stains are common. To remove them, apply a small amount of
dishwashing detergent to the stain and let it soak for five minutes.
Then wash the item in the machine -or use highly carbonated mineral water to help break down fat molecules before washing.

#### 4. Remove Stains from Tonal Cream
>Shaving foam will cope well with the stains.

For tonal cream stains, apply a small amount of shaving gel to the stain and gently scrub with an old toothbrush. Rinse in cold water
and wash as usual.

#### 5. Remove Lipstick Stains
>Use vinegar or toothpaste.

1. Mix water with vinegar (2:1 ratio) and soak the stained area. Wash as usual.
2. Use micellar water on a cotton pad to dab the stain.
3. Apply toothpaste to the stain, let it sit for 10-15 minutes, then wipe with a sponge and rinse.

#### 6. Remove Ink Traces
>Use hairspray

Ink stains on clothes? spray the area with hair spray and wash as usual.

#### 7. Wash Black & White Clothes Together
>Use bay leaves to retain colour.

To wash black and white clothes together, place three bay leaves
in a small bag and add it to the wash. Add one tablespoon of soda to the powder draw to help maintain color.

#### 8. Remove Berry Stains
>Boiling water can help remove stains.

If berry juice stains your clothes, pour boiling water over the stain. The heat will break down the water-soluble pigments, making it easier to wash out.

#### 9. Get Gum Off Your Clothes
>Iron the gum or apply ice.

To remove gum, place a kitchen towel over it and iron for 15-20 seconds. Alternatively, apply ice to the gum to make it easier to remove.
