## Exhaust Setup and Modifications for Optimal Performance

### Proper Exhaust Routing
- **Swap the Exhaust Orientation**: Change the exhaust to prevent it from exiting directly downward. This generates excess heat in the underplate and can lead to a rise in temperature (up to 5 degrees over the desired level). It also increases fuel consumption.
- **Avoid Water Accumulation**: Ensure there's no bending that could trap water or unburnt diesel. Route the exhaust downwards from the unit, then use a 90-degree bend angled slightly downwards outside, allowing proper drainage.

### Hose and Fuel Lines
- **Heat Hose Position**: Install the heat hose at the front to pull heat away from the burner nozzle. This adjustment can optimize fuel consumption, allowing it to burn at the ideal temperature of 163-167Â°C with a fuel-air ratio of 14:1.
- **Use Rigid Fuel Lines**: Consider installing rigid fuel lines, similar to those used in boats. They provide more accurate fueling by eliminating flex and expansion during the pump's operation, resulting in quieter burner operation and prolonged pump life.

### Critical Do's and Don'ts
- **Avoid Filters on the Intake**: Filters can restrict airflow.
- **Limit Bends in Exhaust and Intake**: Excess bends can lead to inefficiencies.
- **Wiring Precautions**: Do not extend wiring without calculating the resistance, and prefer 12V or 24V DC power. Mains power can lead to spikes or cuts, damaging the heater.
- **Pump Positioning**: Research the ideal height and angle for the pump; incorrect positioning can cause fuel line issues.
- **Pump Noise Reduction**: Use a pink rubber cover to dampen pump noise, and ensure it doesn't touch any surrounding surfaces.
- **Tank Recommendations**: Avoid thin, large tanks due to leakage risks. If using a 5-gallon drum, ensure that it has a proper breathable blue lid.

### Maintenance and Replacement Tips
- Genuine setups can handle about **5-6 hard shut downs** before needing part replacement. Keep this in mind for long-term maintenance.
- Always be cautious with installation to prevent future issues with the electronic components, which can be easily damaged during abrupt shutdowns.

Implementing these guidelines will ensure longevity and efficiency, enhancing your experience as a boat builder and operator of these systems.
