Timeline: Key Events in Human History (B.C.)

Prehistoric Era

- 200,000 B.C : Emergence of Homo sapiens in Africa [^1][^4]
- 62,000 B.C : Bow and arrows with stone points are used [^3]
- 30,000 B.C : Cro-Magnon humans thrive, creating cave paintings and spreading across Europe [^6]
- 25,000 B.C : Humans cross the Bering Strait into the Americas[^3][^4]
- 18,000 B.C : Clay pottery and use of raw metals begin[^3]
- 10,000 B.C : Humans reach South America's southernmost point; agriculture begins[^3][^5]


Neolithic Revolution and Early Civilizations

- 8,000 B.C : Domestication of animals and crops in the Fertile Crescent; first domesticated dog found in Idaho (8400 B.C.)[^5][^2]
- 7,000 B.C : Earthquake destroys Jericho[^3]
- 6,500 B.C : Rising seas isolate England from Europe[^3]
- 6,000 B.C : Beer is brewed in the Near East[^3]
- 5,500 B.C : Settled societies emerge in Mesopotamia; start of the Bronze Age[^3][^5]


Bronze Age Innovations

- 4,500 B.C : Introduction of the plough; copper and bronze metallurgy develop[^3][^5]
- 4,242 B.C : First year recorded on the Egyptian calendar[^3]
- 4,000 B.C : Wheel invented; rice farming develops in China[^3]
- 3,400 B.C : Writing begins; ziggurats are built in Mesopotamia[^2][^3]


Early Empires

- 3,100 B.C : Egypt’s 1st Dynasty unites Upper and Lower Egypt[^3]
- 2,750 B.C : King Gilgamesh rules Uruk; Stonehenge build begins (2400 B.C.)[^3][^2]
- 1,792 B.C : Hammurabi reigns in Babylon; Shang dynasty begins in China (1766 B.C.)[^3]
- 1,250–1,200 B.C : Moses leads Israelites out of Egypt (Exodus)[^3]


Iron Age Developments

- 814 B.C : Carthage founded by Phoenicians[^3]
- 776 B.C : First Olympic Games held in Greece[^3]
- 221 B.C –214 B.C : China unified under Shi Huangdi; Great Wall build begins[^3]


Roman Era

- 63–53 B.C : Judea captured by Rome; Battle of Carrhae between Romans and Parthians occurs[^3]
- 44–27 B.C : Julius Caesar assassinated (44 B.C.), Augustus becomes first Roman emperor (27 B.C.)[^3]


Transition to Common Era

- 5 B.C : Birth of Jesus in Bethlehem marks a significant cultural and religious milestone[^3]
